Details About the Event

Ennovate is an absolute blend of Entrepreneurship, Innovation and Technology, where students from every background can come together to innovate and mould their ideas to realistic solutions and progressive business plans.

Ennovate’19 is a Techno-Business event with a tagline ‘Make your work know its worth’. Participants are asked to build a prototype of website/ web application demonstrating the use of any technology and provide innovative solutions for real-world problems through their prototype. Along with the prototype, the teams are also asked to provide a business plan for their prototype.

Gaurang Shetty has been part of the startup ecosystem through RiiDL for some time now and he said that the students were fortunate enough to be in a campus where they have an incubator inside the campus so they can spend some time and learn, shadow there and see how their lives are like.

Statisitcs
1.Prize Money: 1st place – 7000 Rs + Goodies.
2nd place – 5000 Rs
3rd place – 1500 Rs + Goodies.
4th place – 1500 Rs + Goodies.

2.Number of Participants: Total 18 teams participated in the competition from colleges all over Mumbai with major participation from K.J. Somaiya College of Engineering.
3.Duration: The participants were given a time of 15-20 days to build their prototype. The event was held on 5th April 2019 in K.J. Somaiya College of Engineering.
4.Specifics:
Round 1 (Elimination Round) : 5th April 2019
The competition started with the elimination round. Each team were given 7 minutes (5 mins for presenting and 2 mins for Q&A) for Round 1 where they had to pitch their ideas through their prototype.
Round 2 (Final Round) : 5th April 2019
The top 8 selected teams were then given 12 minutes per team (7 mins for presentation and 5 mins for Q&A) for presentation. In this round, participants presented their problem statements, their respective solutions to it using technology and their business models. Our highly recognized panel of judges for this event were:
1. Mr. Rohit Kilam (CIO/ CTO, Aditya Birla Finance Ltd.)
2. Mr. Neil Savant (Founder & CEO, NUOS Home Automation)

6. Sunayana Jadhav (Assistant Professor, Department of Information Technology KJSCE)

Details About the Event

Hack-IoT (Hackathon based on IoT) conducted by BloomBox, Entrepreneurship Cell KJSCE and RiiDL, the aim is to provide a platform to design and create innovative solutions that can directly impact millions of lives.

Statisitcs
1. Cash Prize worth INR 15,000
2.Incubation Facility (RiiDL)
3.FabLab (RiiDL) membership worth INR 6,000
4.Two Months Internship Opportunity at VPS Techub Private Limited
5.Goodies & Gift Voucher from Facebook Developer Circle, Pizza Hut and many more
Round 1 (Elimination Round) : 5th April 2019

Specifics:
1. Speaker Session by Neil Savant (Founder & CEO, NUOS Home Automation) – 30th October 2018.
Topic: Internet of Things: A new growing field
A brief on the real world implementations of IoT and it's modules were given by Mr. Neil Savant. An insight on how having a start-up business is different from a corporate job was also given and a few challenges faced by budding startups were also addressed.
2. Hands on Workshop by Prof. Irfan Siddhavatam – 2nd November 2018.
Irfan Sir explained how to demonstrate a working IoT model. He also gave a brief idea about the judging criteria of the prototypes and some helpful insights on how the teams can make maximum utilization of 30 hours of the hackathon.
3. Speaker Session by Mr. Gaurang Shetty (Chief innovation catalyst and CEO, riidl) – 3rd November 2018.
Topic: How to deliver a business pitch
Mr. Gaurang Shetty gave detailed analysis of start-up community. He explained a few problems faced while launching a product and also how product market compatibility plays a crucial role in making it a success. He threw light on the aspects of a business pitch and how important it is to have a concise, crisp and self-explanatory tagline. He also spoke about elevator pitches and concluded the session by giving insights for an ideal product presentation and business pitch.
4. Hackathon – 3rd November 2018.
The teams were given their components and the Hackathon commenced at 12:30pm on 3rd November, 2018.
5. Submission of Prototypes – 4th November 2018.
The teams stayed back overnight and worked on the prototypes. At 6:30pm the Hackathon was over and the teams submitted their prototypes.
6. Pitching Session – 5th November 2018.
The teams were allotted time slots of 15 minutes each where they had to present their product and propose a business pitch.
7. Closing Ceremony and Results Declaration – 5th November 2018

Duration of Event :-The event was held from 30th October 2018 to 6th November 2018

About

BloomBox is the entrepreneurship cell at K. J. Somaiya College of Engineering. We believe that engineers harbour a latent potential to be the employment generators of tomorrow, pioneering technological and economic growth of the country. BloomBox is intended as a platform to motivate and educate students about entrepreneurship and encourage them to flesh out their ideas in business terms and form global enterprises that combine technology, vision and business acumen. Our aim is to channelize our efforts towards spreading knowledge and inculcating enthusiasm amongst the students by altering their perspective of business in a manner that they shall cherish and shall remain etched in their memory.
